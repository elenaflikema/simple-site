Looking back at this time with my buddy Sandro, it feels like it was so long ago! I think this was in October of 2017(I think). Anyways, he has grown so much since then (his english skills as well as maturity level, physically). I started working with him when he was four years old if I remember correctly. It was a daunting task at first. He knew absolutely no English and my track record with kids wasn't the best at that time.

Our first months were quite monotonous for me. ABC's, memory games, flashcards (the same ones again and again and again). Him being so young, to keep his attention was excruciatingat times. After studying for six months, he memorized the alphabet, his vocabulary began to develop, and his comfort level with me went to another level. Sometimes though, our meetings were a grind!

Sandro and I developed an unbreakable bond at a young age. Much of it having to do with the parallels in our childhoods. I grew up in a divorced family and so does he. I am able to relate to his moods, confusion, and questions without "legitimate" answers. The results of that connection between us has made our english lessons more effective, a sense of camaraderie, as well as genuine friendships between his family and I.

I miss our face-to-face meetings, homemade syrniki-it's like a cutlet made from cottage cheese, milk and so on... delicious desserts from his grandma, chatting with Mama Tanya, and more. I appreciate the fact that we are able to continue his progress and development through video sessions twice a week. I am looking forward to visiting Russia in June and spending time with him and his family at their summer home!

Sandro today and Sandro four years ago are two different people. His English has skyrocketed in the past two years. We speak in English most of the time. I'm quite surprised that he is so engaged in our video sessions. I was a bit skeptical of the idea at first due to his age. He has proven me wrong! I'm so proudof how far he has come and to see the work that I have put into him, the results speak for themselves! Love my extended family!

Take a look at this video of him and I discussing fears and girls. Our topic was about Halloween, what is scary, what are you afraid of. He connects fears to girls at his age. It always gives me a good chuckle!
