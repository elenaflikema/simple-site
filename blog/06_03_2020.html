The photograph above is empowering to say the least. It hypnotized me and left a plethora of questions running through my mind on repeat. It pushed me to reflect on who I am, the things I have done, the choices I have made, and why I am who I am now. What side do you take? Do you align yourself with a group, faction, or party based on your principles and values, or lean in another direction because it is the popular, hip, comfortable, easy, or "acceptable" path to follow?

Time and experience have both taught me that labeling myself, affiliating with a specific group or party has stunted my growth and development in a number of ways. It's easy to fall in and feel a sense of belonging, camaraderie, and peace knowing that all of those around you have the same perspectives and adhere to the same rules, a similar way of life. Fitting in is the easy way, the comfortable decision. Sometimes when we attempt to fit in, we compromise our values and standards, going the wrong way down a one-way street.

Finding independence and freedom is critical to the development of our minds and to achieve success and progress in life. What is independence and freedom to you? To me, it means not categorizing myself by saying, "I'm a neo-right-liberal-blah-blah-blah" or "I'm an ultra- conservative, right-wing protestant" and following others because I don't want to think for myself and have my own opinions and ideas. Being independent to me means thinking for myself and developing my own ideas and opinions without external pressure. Living in a way where I explore all options, opinions, variants, and not letting one person(the manager/controller of a group-who could be a priest, a representative of a party,etc) influence or dictate my belief system, my codex, my lifestyle.

How do you find yourself and your identity? For many, it is not an easy task. Going through different situations, breaking down those barriers which have been built based on stereotypes, fears, and irrational ideas are great steps in the right direction. Stop caring about other peoples' opinions, how people might view you, judge you. Start looking in the mirror and caring about what you think of what you do every day, how you live your life, where you are and where you want to be.

In short

-go against the grain if your conscience tells you it's not your way(even if it isn't comfortable and the feedback you receive might not be the kindest).

-own who you are, embrace your personal views, standards, and principles

-be a wolf, not a sheep(don't follow just because the majority are doing it or because it is trendy)

-always keep an open mind and don't refuse yourself new experiences and opportunities out of fear, because of stereotypes

Join the revolt,

Josh

Grammar notes:

stop+gerund

stop+gerund= to finish the gerund action

He stopped eating dinner at midnight

stop+infinitive=to begin the infinitive action

He stopped to eat dinner at midnight

-there was another action happening before the action "eat"

mean+gerund= in other words/in short

Independence to me means thinking for myself, setting my rules

mean+infinitive=my intention

I mean to wake up every day at 0900

-your intention doesn't mean that it happens, it can end in failure.

-context is everything!
