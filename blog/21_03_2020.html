Not long ago I wrote a piece on our future plans and how many things I am looking forward to experiencing this year. Three weeks ago, I was filled with emotion! I was thrilled and impatient about what was to come. Yesterday evening I had a look at that list and crossed out everything on it. From Elena's birthday trip to Phu Quoc(which should be happening on Monday), to relocating to Bali, and the filming of this TV show(it is still going to happen, just delayed a month or so). Everything has been either called off or pushed back until further notice. For a few, it took the wind out of my sails, mentally.

Life is wild, life is unpredictable. Three weeks ago, I couldn't imagine as like we are now. Bars, cinemas, karaoke joints(extremely popular amongst the locals. Not my cup of tea places where people go to mingle have closed their doors at least temporarily, if not permanently. I've been restricted to my flat more or less. I only go out to get food, supplies and necessary items. I wear a mask everywhere I go, I thoroughly wash my hands more often than Howie Mandel does, I wash my keys, handles on bags, my building card, my sunglasses every time I come home. It sounds crazy, but Howie.....that guy is right! We all need to take some preventative measures at this time to help reduce the spreading of this infection. People say masks don't help. That is a myth. You feel stupid wearing one? You would probably be quite upset if you found out that you contracted the virus through someone who hadn't been wearing a mask. Everyone needs to get on the same page and do the right thing. If we aren't all acting accordingly, this problem will flare up time and time again, resetting the progress we have made!

Us bold brothers stick together

Other changes......the locals have started to treat us foreigners(white people to be more specific) differently. I can only recall a few times in my life where I have felt so judged, been denied services, entry to places because of my skin color. Many locals(of course not all) here now believe that white people are the main cause of the virus in Vietnam. To be fair, they have a right to behave this way to a certain degree. Vietnam had been very well contained until one Vietnamese person came back from Italy or France and a few Europeans entered the country aware of the fact that they had gotten the virus(this is rumored, I cannot verify at this time)! What a selfish, careless act against us all. Since those people have come across the border, the numbers have risen from about 30 to almost 100. The blame cannot besolely placed on them, but they did contribute immensely to the to the global problem we are up against. I was in an elevator yesterday. It stopped on the floor before me and a group of locals were waiting. They stared at me, I looked at them(with masks on mind you), it felt like a standoff, high noon. They just stood there, frozen. The door closed and I continued down the shaft on my own. Out of caution, out of fear, xenophobia, who knows. It's hard to read people at the moment because we are all wearing masks(at least the considerate ones). Reading eyes is much more difficult. Posture and body language tell a lot though. On a number of occasions, a local has turned his/her back to me in a public place(but treat another locals in a much more approachable manner). Parents take their children and shield them from me. I feel like a cancer, a plague, a parasite. I feel so unwelcomed at times. I don't let it make me angry. I don't retaliate. I absorb the mental blow and put up with it with the current situation. I have a great deal of empathy and am an understanding person. Tolerance will always overcome and defeat hatred in the end.

I attempted to order flowers for my wife for a holiday. My Vietnamese friend ordered them(in his language). Twenty minutes later I was back in my flat and the flower company called my phone. I picked up the phone and said, "Hello". Click.... silence. I tried calling back to no avail. A few minutes later I received a message saying, "cancelled, you are a foreigner". I was taken aback. At first I felt insultedand disrespected. It took me a bit to get over it. I called another flower company which was more than happy to help me out! Problem solved. Carry on sergeant. Suck it up.

It's been one hell of a month here. I'm learning a lot about how minorities feel on any given day. Keep your head up, don't get discouraged. Continue to wear a smile while wearing a mask, some people will notice it. Wave at your neighbors while walking down the street in hopes that you will break down those barriers that have been created, and fortified as a result of this virus. I was out on a "supply run" two days ago(sounds so apocalyptic, The Walking Dead-ish I know) and two local kids were intently watching us at a distance. We waved at each other, continued on our way home. Little things like that are inspiring and give us hope throughout all of this,

Peace and Love

Josh

Pay attention to the highlighted words

Past Perfect

had+3rd form/past participle

-this tense is used when we want to state that one action happened before another action in the past(both actions finished in the past too)

-if we are only talking about one past action, there is no need to use past perfect(use past simple)

Example:

He had worked as a teacher until the crisis started (two past finished actions, past perfect is the first action)

He worked as a teacher many years ago (past simple, only one action. We don't need to define which action happened first when there is only one action
