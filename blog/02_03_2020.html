I am very much looking forward to the next few months! I have so many plans for traveling, friends from the USA visiting, and something epic that everyone will be able to watch from the comfort of their homes, broadcast on their televisions in the very near future! I can't say much about it due to disclosure forms and whatnot, but it is going to be a unique, and cool experience to say the least! So many things are happening in the next months, it makes it hard to believe that everything will go according to plan.

April is going to be insane! We are spending the first week of April in Hanoi. A friend is flying in from the USA and we are planning on checking out the Formula 1 race. I've never been to a race like that. It's going to be awesome! A few days after, other friends from the USA are visiting for two weeks. We plan to take them on a tour of southern and central Vietnam. Currently, we are planning a route from our city, to the north, and back again. Many details are involved in this. We haven't visited the north yet, so we are doing some research, figuring out where we are going to be on which dates, in order to book our accommodations .

Over the years, I have told myself that I would do many things, "planned things out" but did not do what I said I was going to do. I've definitely learned from those situations, and the feelings of disappointment in the end when it did not happen. What have I done to reduce the risk of failure and have more success in executing my actions living a more rewarding life?

1. Setting realistic, clear, and achievable goals. Make a short-term plan/long-term plan detailing how you will achieve this. Also, having goals which align with my values and standards. It is excruciatingly difficult to hit your targets if you don't have the motivation, energy, and enthusiasm for them

2. Taking ownership of my words and actions. If you say you are going to do something, do it. Don't talk about doing something because it "sounds cool", it's the "popular thing", it's what someone wants to hear, and so on. Be a doer, not a sayer.

3. Giving myself deadlines! Having goals without deadlines is a trap . Not having a deadline enables a person to procrastinate and push that goal back again and again. You must be able to see the finish line(noun-the end of a race)!

Before moving to Vietnam, many people were overly enthusiastic and adamant about visiting us after we had relocated. Ninety-five percent of those people have pushed those plans back, cancelled, or come up with reasons why they can't come. Changing your perspective a bit, following those three steps listed above, having more willpower , and getting rid of the word "can't" will help to liberate you and provide new experiences and opportunities for you in the future!

Remember, take action. Don't talk about taking action,

Josh

Grammar notes

Gerunds - what is it and when to use?

A gerund is the verb+ing :

to set=setting

to take=taking

to give=giving

A gerund acts as a subject/object of a sentence when one is needed. If you scroll up and look at the three steps, you will see that i began each with a gerund. We cannot begin a statement/answer with a verb. You can only begin a question or a command with a verb.

Plan on + gerund VS. plan + to infinitive

Why do we use a gerund after plan on, but "to infinitive" after plan?

- after a preposition, we use nouns/gerunds

- after the verb plan, if we want to use another verb, it must be in infinitive form (certain list of verbs+infinitive)

There are many more reasons we use gerunds and infinitives. You can check out my videos and find some more help as well as schedule a meeting with me to improve your grammar and English skills!
